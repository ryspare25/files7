# Financial Slack as Group Externality

**Q:** How does financial slack create positive externalities for a group?

**A:** Members who can occasionally cover group costs — short-term (fronting a dinner bill) or long-term (absorbing costs without reimbursement) — make group operation smoother. The covering member bears the full cost; the benefit is diffused across the group. Result: groups chronically underinvest in financial slack.

**Concrete case:** The Berkeley rationalist hub suffers from expensive housing → low financial slack → negative externalities on group functioning, independently of individual circumstances.

*Source: johnswentworth, LessWrong, 29 Jul 2021*
