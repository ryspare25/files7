Chi-sun asked Jan Yu, saying: “Have you, Sir, acquired your military aptitude by study, or is it innate?” [The question raises whether military ability is merely a natural disposition—courage, aggression, physical strength—or whether it can be cultivated through learning. Its implication is important: if military judgment can be studied, then it belongs among the disciplines of responsible government rather than among the instincts of the violent.]
Jan Yu replied: “It has been acquired by study.” [The answer elevates military competence into an intellectual and ethical discipline. Strategy, command, logistics, timing, discipline, and the proper use of force are not presented as spontaneous gifts. They require instruction, reflection, and the formation of judgment.]
