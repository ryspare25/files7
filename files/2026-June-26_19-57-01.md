
    Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman

Two Systems: System 1 (fast, intuitive) vs. System 2 (slow, deliberate). Most decisions are influenced by System 1.

Cognitive Biases: Anchoring, availability heuristic, framing effects, and overconfidence are all explored in depth.

Loss Aversion: We fear losses more than we value equivalent gains.

Prospect Theory: Explains how people make decisions in risky situations—better than classical economics
