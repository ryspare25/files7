The first major claim of the next section is sweeping: recorded human history is described as a history of class struggle. Society, in this account, has not primarily moved through the ideas of great individuals, the decisions of kings, or the accidents of diplomacy. It has moved through conflicts between groups whose positions in the economic order give them opposing interests. Across different historical periods, the names change—freeman and slave, patrician and plebeian, lord and serf, guild-master and journeyman—but the recurring pattern remains: one class possesses power, property, legal privilege, or control over productive activity, while another class is subordinated, exploited, dependent, or excluded.
