# Space Slack as Group Externality

**Q:** How does space slack create positive externalities for a group?

**A:** Groups need space to meet or store things, either short- or long-term. A member with spare space provides this to the whole group. But unless the group *pays* for use of that space, the provider captures only a small fraction of the benefit — leading to systematic underinvestment in space slack.

**Extension (johnswentworth):** Abundant space also creates *social-permissibility slack* — when there is enough space, many activities become possible without requiring permission from others. Dense urban environments eliminate this.

*Source: johnswentworth, LessWrong, 29 Jul 2021*
