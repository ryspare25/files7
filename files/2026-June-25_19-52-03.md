This slide covers the fundamentals of DNS caching and its role in performance optimization.
⚡ Why Caching Matters
Reduces latency: Speeds up website loading times.
Lowers server load: Decreases traffic on DNS servers.
🌐 Where Caching Occurs
Browser cache: Stores your past website visits locally.
OS cache: Keeps records inside system files.
Recursive resolver cache: Saves data at the ISP level.
⏳ Time-To-Live (TTL)
Expiration timer: Defines how long cached data remains valid.
Update control: Determines when systems fetch fresh records.
