Final Reflection: The Power of Subtraction

Another application of via negativa: spend less, live longer is a subtractive strategy.
[The idea of via negativa—improvement by removal—suggests that health can often be enhanced more reliably by eliminating harmful practices than by adding new ones.]

We saw that iatrogenics comes from the intervention bias, via positiva, the propensity to want to do something…
[Doctors and systems tend toward action, even when inaction may be better. This is a cognitive and institutional bias rooted in the desire to feel helpful or avoid blame.]

But let’s do some via negativa here: removing things can be quite a potent (and, empirically, a more effective) strategy.
[By stepping back, removing overtesting, overtreating, overmedicating, we allow natural healing, avoid harm, and increase resilience.]
