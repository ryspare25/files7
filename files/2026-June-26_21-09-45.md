Effective executives, therefore, make effective decisions [decisions that actually solve the right problem and unlock measurable progress].
They make these decisions as a systematic process [not by hunch alone but by an ordered method] with clearly defined elements and in a distinct sequence of steps [a reproducible workflow that others can observe and learn].
But this process bears amazingly little resemblance to what so many books today present as “decision-making” [popular checklists and psychometric tricks often miss the essence].
