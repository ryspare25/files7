There is a break-even point that is easily crossed by panicked doctors and patients: treating the tumor that will not kill you shortens your life—chemotherapy is toxic.
[The point at which the benefit of intervention outweighs the cost is often poorly understood, and fear drives action—leading to overtreatment that reduces life quality and length. Chemotherapy, for example, is inherently toxic and harms healthy tissue.]

We have built up so much paranoia against cancer, looking at the chain backward, an error of logic called affirming the consequent.
[This is a fallacy: just because all people who died of cancer had malignant tumors, it does not mean that all malignant tumors lead to death. This backward reasoning leads to unnecessary interventions.]
