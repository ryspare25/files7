# Game Theory: Always Be Prepared for Confrontation

**Original Post by @incentivising:**

> Game theory proves that you must always be prepared for confrontation. Regardless of how successful, how liked, or how loved you are, you will face opposition. New threats emerge, and former allies won't be driven by the same incentives as you anymore. To find out what drives your enemies and allies, you must look at their past. Remember that behaviors repeat; the past helps reveal what their true intentions might be. Peace is maintained through readiness, not naivety.

This post effectively distills key insights from game theory—particularly repeated games and incentive structures—into practical life advice.

## Core Ideas It Captures Well

### 1. Confrontation Is Inevitable

In non-zero-sum or competitive environments, new players or shifting incentives naturally create opposition.

### 2. History as a Predictor

Past behavior is one of the strongest signals of future actions.

### 3. Peace Through Strength and Readiness

Cooperation is often sustained not by trust alone, but by credible deterrence.

## Dynamic Nature of Allies and Enemies

Relationships are rarely permanent. Incentives change, resources shift, and new opportunities emerge.

## Practical Applications

### Business
- Monitor competitors continuously.
- Understand stakeholder incentives.
- Prepare contingency plans.

### Negotiations
- Evaluate actions rather than promises.
- Maintain leverage and alternatives.

### Relationships
- Pay attention to consistent behavioral patterns.
- Avoid blind trust while remaining cooperative.

### Personal Growth
- Anticipate challenges.
- Build resilience and optionality.

## Key Takeaway

Game theory does not teach paranoia—it teaches realism.

**Peace is not maintained by assuming conflict will never occur. Peace is maintained by being prepared when it does.**
