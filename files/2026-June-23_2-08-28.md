Summary of the page: The author explains that antifragility is not just about surviving randomness, volatility, or stress but thriving and getting better because of it. It contrasts antifragility with resilience, where the resilient endures shocks and remains the same, while the antifragile improves. Taleb gives examples of systems and organisms, like evolution and human bodies, that benefit from stress and randomness.

Antifragility is also discussed as a key concept in dealing with uncertainty and complexity, as systems that are antifragile grow and adapt in the face of disorder. Taleb criticizes modern society for suppressing randomness and volatility, particularly through top-down policies, which he argues weakens systems and makes them more fragile.

He also touches upon the notion that complex systems thrive under the right amount of stress and disorder, proposing that innovation and discovery come from bottom-up tinkering rather than formal design.
