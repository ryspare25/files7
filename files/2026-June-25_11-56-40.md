In "Thinking Fast and Slow", there's that deep dive into loss aversion and how people fear losing more than they enjoy winning. For the mob, losing turf or control isn't just a financial hit—it's losing face. That's even worse. Same thing in business; no CEO wants to show weakness. So instead of an all-out war, where both sides lose more, they split the pie. Everyone stays rich and alive. Everyone fears losing more than the risk of bloodshed.
