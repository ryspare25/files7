They know that the most time-consuming step in the process is not making the decision but putting it into effect [execution swallows more calendar than analysis].
Unless a decision has “degenerated into work” it is not a decision; it is at best a good intention [only when tasks, owners, and deadlines exist does a decision breathe].
This means that, while the effective decision itself is based on the highest level of conceptual understanding [vision must be lofty], the action to carry it out should be as close as possible to the working level and as simple as possible [implementation must be grounded and streamlined]. (principle, gensoku)
