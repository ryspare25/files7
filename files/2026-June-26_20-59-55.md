As a case study, consider mammograms. It has been shown that administering them to women over forty on an annual basis does not lead to an increase in life expectancy (at best; it could even lead to a decrease).
[While the intuitive assumption is that early detection saves lives, the data shows that routine screening may not deliver the promised net benefit in longevity. Instead, it may introduce other risks that offset or even outweigh the benefits of detecting certain cancers early.]
