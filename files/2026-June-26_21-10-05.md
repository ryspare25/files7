Effective executives do not make a great many decisions [they avoid death by a thousand micro-choices]. <shibō> (overkill, shibō)
They concentrate on the important ones [those few high-leverage calls that set cascading events in motion].
They try to think through what is strategic and generic, rather than “solve problems” [they hunt root causes and enduring patterns, not transient symptoms].
They try to make the few important decisions on the highest level of conceptual understanding [framing the issue in big-picture terms, not tactical trivia].
They try to find the constants in a situation [unchanging principles that must be honored regardless of noise].
