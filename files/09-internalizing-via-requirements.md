# Internalizing Slack Externalities via Explicit Requirements

**Q:** What is the alternative to status rewards for internalizing slack externalities?

**A:** Groups can **require** slack from members, either explicitly or through de facto exclusion:

- *Implicit:* Low-slack members get excluded from group activities (meeting is held without them; they're not invited to high-stress competitions).
- *Explicit:* The National Guard formally requires members to be able to drop everything and respond full-time to emergencies.

**Implication for group design:** Where slack is mission-critical, explicit requirements outperform implicit status incentives, because the status mechanism is too illegible and weak to reliably produce the high-slack equilibrium.

*Source: johnswentworth, LessWrong, 29 Jul 2021*
