Closing Argument

Ladies and gentlemen:

We are asked to believe that alternative resources were added, overlapping capabilities were introduced, travel engagement diminished, and a higher-level directive altered the staffing landscape—and that none of it means anything.

That is not how organizations behave.

Companies do not spend money creating redundancy without purpose.

Companies do not build alternatives without valuing alternatives.

Companies do not reduce dependence accidentally.

The strongest interpretation of the evidence is not that the yeeting has already occurred.

It is that the groundwork for a future yeeting is being laid.

And in corporate life, the groundwork is often the most important part.

Because by the time the official announcement arrives, the outcome has usually been decided long before.
