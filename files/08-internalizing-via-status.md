# Internalizing Slack Externalities via Status

**Q:** What is the standard mechanism by which groups partially internalize the externalities of member slack?

**A:** The econ-101 answer is to reward slack-holders. In practice this rarely takes the form of monetary payment; instead groups award **social status** — implicitly and illegibly — to members who provide space, cover costs, or absorb coordination burden.

**Limitation (dominicq):** Time-slack is poorly rewarded even by status. The person who says "whenever's fine" is taken for granted, not elevated. This suggests the status mechanism is *insufficient* to reliably select for high-slack members — explicit requirements may be necessary.

*Source: johnswentworth / dominicq, LessWrong, 29 Jul 2021*
