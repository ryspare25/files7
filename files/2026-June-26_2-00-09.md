In a second-price, sealed-bid auction, the highest bidder wins the item but pays a price equal to the bid of the second-highest bidder. [— the winner's payment is determined by someone else's information, not their own bid — a structurally elegant decoupling of the winning condition from the pricing condition] A second-price rule can be extremely useful for eliciting truthful bids, as we will see in Section 4. [— dominant strategy result: bidding your true value weakly dominates all other strategies, because shading down risks losing without saving money, and shading up risks winning at a loss] Such an auction is often termed a Vickrey auction after the Nobel-prize-winning economist who first noted this particular characteristic.
