⚙️ The Four Types of DNS Servers
Recursive Resolvers: These servers act as the "librarians" of the internet. They receive the initial query from your web browser, check their local cache first, and if the answer is missing, contact the other three types of DNS servers on your behalf to fetch the IP address.

Root Name Servers: This is the very first step in the external search path. Root servers do not map specific domains to IP addresses; instead, they read the end of the query (like .com, .org, or .net) and direct the recursive resolver to the correct Top-Level Domain (TLD) server.

TLD Name Servers: These servers manage specific domain extensions (such as .com or .net). A .com TLD server keeps track of all registries under that extension and points the resolver directly to the specific authoritative nameserver for that exact website.

Authoritative Name Servers: This is the final stop in the resolution sequence. These servers hold the actual, official DNS records for the specific domain name. They provide the final IP address back to the recursive resolver, allowing your browser to successfully connect to the web server.
