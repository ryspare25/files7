The Godfather Rule – Retroactive Approval

Here’s the kicker: once you ratify something, it’s like you approved it from day one.

Example:
Imagine Tony the Torch (your second-in-command) shakes hands on a deal to offload some stolen diamonds. He had no authority, but the family later backs him up. Now the poor bastard who tried to back out of the deal? Too bad—he’s locked in, even if he changed his mind.

That’s what happened in Bolton Partners v. Lambert—some corporate suit tried to back out of a deal after the fact, but the court said, “Too late, buddy, you’re stuck with it.”
