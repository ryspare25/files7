Under this modern arrangement, the bourgeoisie owns the means of social production: factories, machinery, workshops, land used commercially, transport systems, capital, and the institutions through which goods are produced and exchanged. The proletariat, by contrast, owns no comparable productive property. Workers must sell their labor power—their time, energy, skill, and capacity to work—in order to obtain wages and survive. This is the central relationship the manifesto identifies: capital employs labor, and labor depends upon capital for access to the means of life.
