Increasing Profit by Price Discrimination: A firm can increase its profit by price discriminating [charging each buyer or buyer-group a tailored price rather than one “posted” uniform price] by charging different prices to different customers based on their willingness to pay [measured or inferred maximum reservation price]. This allows the firm to capture more consumer surplus [the gap between what customers were prepared to pay and what they actually hand over under a single price], converting it into additional profit [extra producer surplus above what would be earned under uniform pricing].
