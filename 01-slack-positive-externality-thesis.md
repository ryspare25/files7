# Slack Creates Positive Externalities for Groups

**Q:** Why does personal slack systematically benefit others more than the slack-holder?

**A:** The person holding slack controls how much to create, but most of the benefit flows to the group — e.g. a flexible schedule enables the group to accommodate *less*-flexible members. Since the slack-holder captures only a fraction of the benefit, individuals are incentivized to maintain *less* slack than is socially optimal.

**Corollary:** Groups will generally be less functional than they could be, because their members are underincentivized to maintain slack.

*Source: johnswentworth, LessWrong, 29 Jul 2021*
