# Emotional Slack as Group Externality

**Q:** How does lack of emotional slack propagate through a group?

**A:** If even one or two members lack emotional slack, they can block group renegotiation of norms. The group gets locked into whatever rules were set under prior conditions, regardless of whether those rules still make sense.

**Illustrative case (Raemon):** Berkeley group houses stayed in over-paranoid COVID lockdown rules for the entire year — not because the rules were correct, but because members lacked "the spare energy and emotional skills to change S1 attitudes while the crisis was still kinda ongoing."

**Mechanism:** Emotional slack is necessary to evaluate whether current group policies are still optimal. Without it, path-dependence dominates.

*Source: johnswentworth, LessWrong, 29 Jul 2021*
