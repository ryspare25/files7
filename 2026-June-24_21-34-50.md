Modern bourgeois society, according to the manifesto, emerged from the destruction of feudal society. The bourgeoisie—the modern capitalist class—overthrew or weakened the old feudal order through commerce, industry, urban growth, political revolution, and the expansion of markets. But the authors insist that this transformation did not abolish class conflict. It changed its form. The old estates and legal ranks were gradually replaced by a society increasingly organized around ownership of capital and the sale of labor.
