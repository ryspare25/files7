# Status Rewards Are Insufficient for Time-Slack

**Q:** Does the implicit status reward for slack work equally well across all slack flavors?

**A:** No. Time-slack is particularly under-rewarded. The person who says "whenever works for me" is taken for granted, not elevated in status — the benefit is too invisible and diffuse for the group to legibly attribute and reward.

**Contrast:** Financial and space slack are more *visible* (you can see who owns the house, who picked up the check) and thus more likely to generate status returns.

**Implication:** Explicit group policies requiring time-slack (e.g. prohibiting fully-packed schedules, reserving buffer time) are more effective than relying on implicit status incentives for this particular flavor.

*(dominicq, LessWrong, 29 Jul 2021)*
