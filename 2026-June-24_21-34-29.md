These struggles are sometimes open: rebellions, revolutions, strikes, civil wars, uprisings, and political confrontations. At other times they are hidden within ordinary social life: unequal laws, inherited status, debt, dependence on employers, restrictions on movement, unequal access to land, and institutions that make domination appear natural or permanent. The authors argue that such conflicts do not simply disappear through compromise. Historically, they culminate either in a broad reorganization of society—where old institutions are broken and new relations of power emerge—or in the mutual destruction and decline of the classes locked in conflict.
